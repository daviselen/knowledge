# Project Initiation Playbook

> How Davis Elen begins client work with clarity, alignment, and intentionality. This playbook ensures every project starts with shared understanding of objectives, roles, timelines, and deliverables.

## Purpose

A well-executed project kickoff prevents misalignment, scope creep, rework, and missed deadlines. This playbook establishes the Davis Elen standard for starting work right.

## The Project Initiation Cycle

Project initiation happens in three phases:

1. **Internal Alignment** — DE team aligns before talking to the client
2. **Client Kickoff** — Strategic, collaborative conversation with the client
3. **Team Mobilization** — All DE disciplines understand their role and timeline

---

## Phase 1: Internal Alignment (1-2 weeks before client kickoff)

### Who Participates

Required:
- Account Lead (primary client contact)
- Strategy Lead
- Creative Director (or relevant discipline head)

As appropriate:
- Media Director (if media planning is part of the scope)
- Digital/Production Lead (if relevant to the project)
- Multicultural/Research specialist (if client requires this expertise)

### Pre-Kickoff Meeting

**Objective:** DE team reaches shared understanding of the project scope, objectives, and strategy before involving the client.

**Timing:** Minimum 1 week before client kickoff

**Duration:** 60-90 minutes

**Agenda:**

1. **Project Overview** (10 min)
   - What is the client asking us to do?
   - What's the business problem they're trying to solve?
   - What's the budget and timeline?

2. **Client Context** (15 min)
   - How long have we worked with this client?
   - What do we know about their business, brand, and markets?
   - What's worked well in past work? What's been challenging?
   - Who are the key stakeholders and decision-makers?
   - What's their communication style and approval process?

3. **Initial Strategic Thinking** (20 min)
   - What are the likely business objectives?
   - What are we trying to move (awareness? consideration? conversion? loyalty)?
   - Who is the audience?
   - What's the competitive context?
   - What constraints or guardrails do we need to know about?

4. **Scope & Deliverables** (15 min)
   - What disciplines are involved? (strategy, creative, media, production, digital, etc.)
   - What is the primary deliverable?
   - Are there secondary deliverables?
   - What's NOT in scope?

5. **Timeline & Resources** (10 min)
   - When does the client need what?
   - What's the launch/delivery date?
   - How many rounds of revisions are built in?
   - Who's leading each discipline?
   - What resources do we need to allocate?

6. **Open Questions** (10 min)
   - What do we need to ask the client to clarify?
   - What information are we missing?
   - What assumptions are we making?

### Pre-Kickoff Deliverable

The Strategy Lead should prepare a **Pre-Kickoff Brief** (1-2 pages):

- Client background and context
- Project scope and deliverables
- Preliminary business objectives and strategic direction
- Key questions for the client
- Timeline and resource plan
- Known constraints or considerations

This brief becomes the foundation for the kickoff meeting.

## Phase 2: Client Kickoff (Strategic Discovery Meeting)

### Purpose

The client kickoff is a strategic, collaborative conversation. It's not a presentation—it's a discovery and alignment session. Both DE and the client should leave with shared understanding of the business challenge, strategic direction, and what success looks like.

### Timing

Ideally 1-2 weeks before creative work begins, to allow time for strategy development and internal planning.

### Who Participates

**From Davis Elen:**
- Account Lead (facilitator, primary relationship)
- Strategy Lead (strategic thinking, questions)
- Creative Director or relevant discipline lead (understands the creative brief and can ask informed questions)
- Media Director (if media planning is in scope)

**From Client:**
- Primary contact / project sponsor
- Brand manager or marketing lead
- Key decision-maker(s)
- Subject matter experts as relevant (product, sales, research, etc.)

**Note:** The client should be invited to bring their full team. More voices = better understanding. If the client can only send one person, that's a signal that this project may lack internal alignment on their side—something to address early.

### Meeting Structure

**Duration:** 90 minutes

**Format:** Collaborative conversation, not presentation

**Location:** Video or in-person, depending on relationship and scope

### Pre-Meeting Prep for DE Team

- Review the Pre-Kickoff Brief
- Prepare a one-page agenda and send to the client 2-3 days in advance
- Prepare key questions (see below)
- Bring examples of relevant past work (if helpful for context)
- Have the Creative Brief template ready (to be filled during or after the meeting)

### Meeting Agenda

#### 1. Welcome & Context (5 min)

Account Lead opens with:
- Thank you for the opportunity
- Brief overview of the meeting objectives: "We want to make sure we fully understand your business challenge, your audience, and what success looks like. This is a conversation, not a presentation."
- How the session will be structured
- Next steps after the meeting

#### 2. Client's Business Challenge (20 min)

**Facilitated by Account Lead. Strategy Lead takes notes.**

Key questions to ask:

- **What problem are we solving?** What's changed in your business or market that prompted this project?
- **What's the business objective?** What do you want to achieve? (increase sales, build awareness, drive consideration, strengthen brand loyalty, etc.)
- **How will you measure success?** What metrics matter to you?
- **What's the context?** What's happening in your market, with your competitors, with your customers?
- **What constraints should we know about?** Budget, timeline, brand guidelines, internal politics, etc.

*Listen more than you talk. The client's own words matter.*

#### 3. Brand & Audience (20 min)

**Facilitated by Strategy Lead.**

- **Brand positioning:** How do you position your brand? What do you stand for?
- **Brand personality:** How does your brand speak? What's your tone and attitude?
- **Target audience:** Who are we talking to? (demographics, psychographics, behaviors, motivations)
- **Audience insights:** What do they care about? What are their pain points? Why would they choose your brand?
- **Competitive set:** Who else are they considering? What's your advantage?

#### 4. Campaign Strategy (15 min)

**Facilitated by Strategy Lead and Creative Director.**

- **The big idea/strategic direction:** Is there a strategic direction you're already thinking about, or are we developing that together?
- **Key message:** What's the single most important thing we need to communicate?
- **Campaign scope:** What channels/disciplines are we using? (advertising, digital, social, PR, events, etc.)
- **Timeline:** When do we need to launch? Are there phases?

#### 5. Team & Process (15 min)

**Facilitated by Account Lead.**

- **Approval process:** How do decisions get made internally? Who needs to approve what?
- **Feedback:** How does the client like to give feedback? (meetings, email, document comments?)
- **Revisions:** How many rounds of creative revisions are expected?
- **Communication:** How often should we check in? Weekly? As-needed?
- **Key contacts:** Who's the primary contact? Who else should we loop in?

#### 6. Next Steps & Expectations (5 min)

- Recap what we heard
- Outline what DE will do next (strategy development, creative concepts, etc.)
- Timeline for next deliverable (creative brief, strategy presentation, concepts, etc.)
- When we'll reconnect

### Post-Kickoff: Notes & Alignment

Immediately after the meeting:

1. **Strategy Lead** synthesizes notes into the Creative Brief (see Creative Brief Standard, below)
2. **Account Lead** sends a thank-you email summarizing what was discussed and confirming next steps
3. **DE team** meets internally to review notes and ensure alignment before beginning creative work

## Phase 3: Team Mobilization

### Internal Alignment Meeting (All Disciplines)

**When:** After strategy is locked, before creative work begins

**Who:** All disciplines involved in the project (creative, media, digital, production, etc.)

**Duration:** 60 minutes

**Purpose:** Ensure every team member understands the strategic direction, their role, the timeline, and how their work connects to other disciplines.

**Agenda:**

1. **Strategic Brief** — Strategy Lead presents the business objective, audience, key message, and strategic direction (15 min)
2. **Discipline-Specific Briefs** — Each discipline lead explains their specific scope, deliverables, and timeline (20 min)
3. **Dependencies & Handoffs** — Map how work flows between disciplines (10 min)
4. **Questions & Alignment** — Ensure everyone is clear on their role and the overall direction (10 min)
5. **Kick-off!** — Confirm everyone knows their next step and when things are due (5 min)

### Work Plan & Timeline

The Account Lead (or Project Manager) creates a detailed work plan documenting:

- All deliverables and due dates
- Who's responsible for each deliverable
- Dependencies and handoffs between disciplines
- Client review and approval dates
- Launch/delivery date

This work plan is shared with the client and internally, so everyone has a shared understanding of the timeline.

## Quality Checkpoints

### Red Flags in Project Initiation

Stop and realign if you notice:

- **Unclear objectives.** If the client can't articulate what they're trying to achieve, strategy work is premature. Ask clarifying questions.
- **Misaligned stakeholders.** If key decision-makers aren't in the room or aren't aligned, you're setting up for revision cycles. Address this early.
- **Scope creep signals.** Vague deliverables, unlimited revisions, or "we want options" often mean the brief isn't tight enough. Push for clarity.
- **Constrained timeline.** If the timeline doesn't allow for proper strategy and creative development, negotiate or reset expectations early.
- **No clear success metric.** If the client can't define how they'll measure success, you can't deliver strategically. Work with them to define metrics.

### Pre-Kickoff Brief Checklist

Before meeting with the client, confirm:

- ✅ Client context and history documented
- ✅ Project scope and deliverables are clear
- ✅ Timeline is realistic for the scope
- ✅ Key questions prepared
- ✅ All DE disciplines understand their role
- ✅ All internal stakeholders have been looped in
- ✅ Budget and resource constraints understood

### Creative Brief Checklist

Before creative work begins, confirm:

- ✅ Business objective is clear and measurable
- ✅ Target audience is well-defined
- ✅ Key message/strategic direction is locked
- ✅ Deliverables and timeline are documented
- ✅ Approval process is understood
- ✅ Success metrics are defined
- ✅ All DE disciplines have reviewed and agreed on the brief

## Common Scenarios & How to Handle Them

### Scenario: Client wants to start creative before strategy is locked

**Your response:** "We've learned that skipping strategy leads to rework and misalignment. We'd rather spend a week getting strategy right than several weeks redoing creative. Let's spend the next week validating the strategy, then move quickly on creative."

### Scenario: Client can only attend kickoff with one person

**Your response:** "We want to make sure we understand everyone's perspective on this project. Can we schedule follow-up conversations with [marketing lead, product manager, sales lead]? It'll save us time in revisions."

### Scenario: Client isn't clear on business objectives

**Your response:** "Before we jump into creative, let's make sure we're aligned on what success looks like. What would need to change in your business for this campaign to be considered successful? [listen] OK, so if we could move that metric by X%, would that feel like a win?"

### Scenario: Client wants unlimited revisions

**Your response:** "Our experience is that 2-3 rounds of revisions lead to the best creative because we're refining direction, not starting over. We'll build in 2 rounds. If additional revisions are needed, we can discuss and adjust the timeline and budget accordingly."

## Key Principles

- **Clarity beats speed.** An extra week of alignment saves several weeks of rework.
- **The brief is strategy work.** Don't delegate brief-writing to account management alone. Strategy and creative leads must be involved.
- **The client is the expert.** They know their business. Your job is to ask good questions and synthesize their knowledge into strategic direction.
- **Document everything.** The Creative Brief is your reference document. If it's not in writing, it doesn't count.
- **Align internally first.** Before presenting to the client, make sure DE is aligned. Mixed messages kill projects.
- **Set expectations early.** Timelines, revision rounds, approval process, communication cadence—say it all at the beginning, not when issues arise.
