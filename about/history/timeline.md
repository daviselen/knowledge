# Davis Elen History Timeline

> A working timeline documenting the evolution of the organization that became Davis Elen.

## Historical Overview

Davis Elen's history spans more than a century of advertising, beginning with founder Henry Meyers in 1925 and continuing through multiple generations of leadership.

The agency's historical lineage is:

Henry Meyers Company (1925)
→ Davis, Johnson, Anderson & Colombatto (1959)
→ Davis, Johnson, Mogul & Colombatto (1961)
→ Davis, Ball & Colombatto (198X)
→ Davis Elen (1996)

Modern company materials identify 1948 as the agency's founding year. However, earlier agency history materials identify 1925 as the founding year and provide a continuous organizational narrative from Henry Meyers through the present-day Davis Elen organization.

## 1925 — Henry Meyers Company

The earliest documented predecessor in Davis Elen's lineage is the Henry Meyers Company, founded in 1925.

Earlier versions of Davis Elen's website referenced 1925 as the agency's founding date. At least one source also lists the year as 1927.

## 1948 — Official Founding Date

Modern Davis Elen communications identify 1948 as the agency's founding year.

The specific event that caused 1948 to become the recognized founding date remains under investigation.

Possible explanations may include:

* Organizational restructuring
* Ownership transition
* Incorporation milestone
* Agency reorganization

No conclusion should be recorded until supported by additional primary sources.

### 1952 - Robert Davis Joins

Robert Davis joined the agency in 1951/2 and quickly moved up.

## 1956 - Merger and Return to Independence

In December 1956, it was announced that the Mayers Company would merge with Cunningham & Walsh (NYC & Chicago) as well as Brisacher, Wheeler & Staff (SF) to form a large, national advertising agency. The marriage was not to last, however, as only a little over a year later, Cunningham & Walsh would exercise their six-month cancellation clause. On September 30, 1958, the merger was over and the Henry Mayers Company went back to being independent.

## 1958 — Ownership Transition

In late 1958, Henry Meyers sold most of his ownership interest in the company to employees who became partners and continued the agency under a new ownership structure. On January 1, 1959, the agency's name was changed to Davis, Johnson, Anderson & Colombatto.

Bob Davis took over leadership and partnered with Robert "Collie" Colombatto, his former roommate at UC Berkeley. Bob served as President during "Even Days," while Collie served as President during "Odd Days," creating a distinctive shared leadership tradition.

During this era, the agency developed a reputation for creative, entrepreneurial, and effective advertising work.

## 1961 - Merger

Los Angeles-based Davis, Johnson, Andersen & Colombatto announced a merger with New York-based Mogul, Williams & Saylor on October 9, 1961. Upon completion of the stock transfer on November 1, the name of the West Coast agency became Davis, Johnson, Mogul & Colombatto.

## 1962 - Robert M. Davis Becomes President

At 36, Mr. Davis was among the youngest advertising agency presidents.

## 1963 - Henry Mayers Retires

Mr. Mayers served as board chairman from 1959 until his retirement in 1963.

## 1978 — Mark Davis Joins the Agency

Mark Davis began his career with the agency in 1975.

He would later become Chairman and CEO, continuing the family leadership tradition established by Henry Meyers and Bob Davis.

## 1988 — Davis, Ball & Colombatto Era

In the early 1980s, leadership transitioned to Mark Davis and Brad Ball.

The two continued the tradition of shared agency leadership, and, in December 1988, the agency changed its name to Davis, Ball & Colombatto.

## 1996 — Davis Elen Name

In the 1995, Brad Ball departed to become head of marketing for McDonald's in the United States.

Bob Elen returned to the agency after a nineteen-year hiatus and became Mark Davis's partner. Bob Elen had originally started his career with the agency in the late 1960s.

The agency adopted the name Davis Elen in 1996.

## Modern Era

Today, Davis Elen operates as an independent advertising agency offering integrated strategy, creative, media, digital, production, and multicultural marketing services.

The agency maintains offices across the United States and serves national brands, particularly in retail and consumer categories.

The agency's historical philosophy emphasizes:

* Being large enough to meet client needs
* Being small enough to provide personal attention
* Being independent enough to remain accountable directly to clients

The agency is not part of a publicly traded advertising holding company.
