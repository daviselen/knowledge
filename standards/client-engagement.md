# Client Engagement Quick Reference

> A one-page reference for the Davis Elen process: how we start projects, develop strategy, deliver work, and maintain relationships.

## The Process at a Glance

### Phase 1: Project Initiation (1-2 weeks)

**Internal Alignment (Pre-Kickoff)**
- Account Lead, Strategy Lead, Creative Director meet
- Review client context and preliminary strategy
- Prepare key questions
- Create Pre-Kickoff Brief

**Client Kickoff (90 min meeting)**
- Understand business objective and challenges
- Define target audience and key message
- Establish strategic direction
- Clarify timeline, deliverables, approval process
- Confirm team and communication norms

**Output:** Creative Brief (approved by client and all DE disciplines)

---

### Phase 2: Strategy Development (1-2 weeks)

**Strategy Team Works**
- Develop strategic insight and positioning
- Create creative direction and messaging strategy
- Outline approach and deliverables
- Prepare strategy presentation or brief

**Internal Alignment (All Disciplines)**
- Strategy Lead presents strategic direction
- Each discipline confirms their scope and timeline
- Map dependencies and handoffs
- Confirm work plan and deadlines

**Output:** Creative Brief locked, work plan confirmed, all disciplines aligned

---

### Phase 3: Creative Development (Variable)

**Creative Teams Execute**
- Concept development
- Design and copywriting
- Production and asset creation
- Internal quality checks

**Ongoing Communication**
- Weekly check-ins with client (brief email or call)
- Share progress proactively
- Flag issues early
- Get feedback as you go (don't save surprises for final review)

**Output:** Creative concepts or final deliverables, ready for review

---

### Phase 4: Client Presentation & Revision

**Presentation Meeting**
- Present work with strategic rationale (not just the files)
- Explain how concepts solve the strategic challenge
- Listen to feedback
- Document what was approved, what needs revision

**Revision Process**
- Round 1: Exploratory (clarify feedback, incorporate direction)
- Round 2: Refinement (polish based on clear feedback)
- If needed: Flag new direction as scope change, not revision

**Output:** Approved creative assets

---

### Phase 5: Production & Launch

**Execution**
- Production begins (video, copywriting, media planning, etc.)
- Manage vendor relationships
- Asset delivery and approvals
- Campaign launch or delivery

**Ongoing Communication**
- Keep client updated on production status
- Flag any issues immediately
- Deliver final assets on time

**Output:** Launched campaign or delivered deliverables

---

### Phase 6: Post-Campaign Review

**Campaign Retrospective (Internal)**
- How did the creative perform?
- Did we hit our strategic objectives?
- What would we do differently?
- What did we learn?

**Client Debrief**
- Share performance data or feedback
- Discuss what worked and learnings
- Discuss next steps and future opportunity

**Output:** Learnings documented, relationship strengthened

## Key Documents

| Document | Purpose | Timing |
|----------|---------|--------|
| **Pre-Kickoff Brief** | DE team alignment before client meeting | 1 week before client kickoff |
| **Creative Brief** | Strategic direction for all creative work | Locked before creative begins |
| **Work Plan** | Timeline, deliverables, responsibilities, approvals | Locked at start of project |
| **Revision Brief** | Clarifies feedback direction before revising | After each client feedback |
| **Campaign Retrospective** | Post-launch analysis and learnings | After campaign launches |

## Communication Checklist

**Weekly (Ongoing):**
- ✅ Brief email update (5 min to write, 5 min for client to read)
- ✅ Share progress, flag issues, confirm next step

**At Deliverable Review:**
- ✅ Strategic context (why we made these choices)
- ✅ Clear presentation (let work speak)
- ✅ Document feedback (don't assume you understood)
- ✅ Confirm next steps in writing

**Monthly/Quarterly (Strategic):**
- ✅ Conversation about broader business and market
- ✅ Share category insights or emerging opportunities
- ✅ Discuss how our work fits into their strategy

**Annually:**
- ✅ Formal business review
- ✅ Discuss what's working, what could improve
- ✅ Plan priorities for next year

## Red Flags & How to Respond

| Flag | Response |
|------|----------|
| **Client can't define success metrics** | Ask: "What would need to change for this to be successful?" Keep asking until they're clear |
| **Vague approval process** | Clarify: "Who needs to approve what? How long does approval typically take? What happens if we disagree?" |
| **Unlimited revisions** | Set expectation: "We've built in 2 revision rounds. Additional rounds would extend timeline/budget" |
| **Scope creep requests** | Clarify: "Is this part of the original scope, or a new request? If new, let's discuss how it affects timeline/budget" |
| **Missed deadlines** | Flag immediately: "We're going to miss the [date] deadline. Here's the revised date and how it affects your timeline" |
| **Approval delays** | Check in: "We're waiting on approval to move forward. When can you get us feedback?" Escalate if necessary |
| **Mixed feedback** | Clarify: "We got different feedback from different people. Help us understand the priority direction" |
| **Client unhappy** | Listen: "I hear you're not happy with [X]. Help me understand what's not working." Don't defend, just listen |

## Creative Brief Checklist

Before presenting creative work, confirm the brief has:

- ✅ **One-sentence business objective.** Clear and measurable
- ✅ **Strategic insight.** Something true about the audience that makes our approach make sense
- ✅ **Specific target audience.** Demographics, psychographics, behaviors
- ✅ **Key message.** The one thing we need to communicate
- ✅ **Tone & personality.** How our work sounds and feels
- ✅ **Creative direction.** Our strategic approach
- ✅ **Deliverables.** What we're making and specs
- ✅ **Success criteria.** How we measure if it worked
- ✅ **Constraints.** Brand guidelines, timeline, budget, guardrails
- ✅ **Client approval.** Has the client reviewed and agreed?

## Presentation Tips

**When Presenting Work to the Client:**

1. **Start with strategy.** "Here's the challenge we're solving and our strategic approach..."
2. **Explain the thinking.** "We chose this direction because..." Defend your work with strategy, not opinion
3. **Present with confidence.** You've thought about this. Show it
4. **Shut up and listen.** After you present, wait for their response. Don't fill silence with talking
5. **Take notes.** Write down their feedback. Don't try to remember it later
6. **Clarify before defending.** If feedback is vague, ask questions: "When you say X, what do you mean?"
7. **Don't argue.** They might be right. Consider their perspective
8. **Confirm next steps.** "Here's what we heard, here's what we'll do next, here's when we'll show you"

## Response Time Standards

| Situation | Target Response Time |
|-----------|----------------------|
| Urgent client request | Same day |
| Non-urgent question | 24 hours |
| Feedback on deliverable | 48 hours |
| Weekly check-in | By end of week |
| Approval request | 5 business days (confirm deadline with client) |
| New project inquiry | 24 hours |

## Relationship Health Check

**Quarterly, ask yourself:**

- [ ] How often are we talking to this client? (Should be weekly minimum)
- [ ] Are we bringing strategic ideas to the table? (Or just executing?)
- [ ] Do they know multiple people at DE? (Not just one person)
- [ ] Have we had feedback about the partnership? (Formal or informal)
- [ ] Are we delivering on our commitments? (On time, on budget, on strategy)
- [ ] Do they seem engaged and enthusiastic? (Or checking out?)
- [ ] Have they introduced us to other stakeholders or opportunities?
- [ ] Are we still learning about their business and market?

If you answer "no" to most of these, the relationship needs attention.
