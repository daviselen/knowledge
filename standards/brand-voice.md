# Davis Elen Advertising — Company Voice Guide

**Last Updated:** July 2026  
**Purpose:** This guide ensures all communications reflect Davis Elen's authentic voice—direct, confident, and deeply rooted in decades of delivering results.

---

## Core Voice Attributes

### **Relentless, Independent & Courageous**
Your voice is unapologetically honest. You don't hide behind corporate-speak or trend-chasing jargon. You acknowledge what clients need, even when it's different from what they expected. You challenge the status quo within reason, always grounded in business reality.

### **Results-Driven**
Every statement points toward tangible outcomes. Not "we're creative," but "cut-through, targeted, results-driven work." You speak the language of ROI, growth, and impact. Avoid empty promises; speak to what you deliver.

### **Paradoxical & Clever**
You embrace contradictions that feel true: "Think Inside the Box," "Thinking That Matters." These work because they're clever without being clever *for its own sake*. They make a point. You're witty but never at the expense of clarity.

### **Direct Yet Warm**
Short sentences mixed with flowing prose. You talk *with* people, not *at* them. You acknowledge challenges candidly ("clients who recognize they have their own unique set of boundaries") without being condescending. Partnership language comes naturally.

### **Deeply Experienced**
Decades of experience matter. You reference it not as bragging but as proof of evolution and staying power. Long-term client relationships (Toyota, McDonald's, decades) aren't thrown in as credentials—they're evidence of mutual growth and respect.

### **What You Say vs. How You Say It**

❌ *"We leverage synergistic solutions to maximize stakeholder engagement."*

❌ *"Our team is passionate about excellence."*  
✅ *"A shared passion to do great work need only be the beginning. When we come together for less expected reasons, the time spent in these other pursuits can be just as valuable, perhaps even more so."*

## Writing Guidelines

### **Structure & Pacing**
- Use short, declarative statements to establish confidence.
- Follow with longer, more reflective sentences to show depth.
- Mix practical facts with human truth.

✅ Example:  
*"We're Davis Elen and we create advertising for clients who recognize they have their own unique set of challenges and nevertheless expect cut-through, targeted, results-driven work. Not a problem. That's exactly what sets Davis Elen apart."*

### **Avoid These**
- Corporate jargon ("leverage," "synergize," "next-generation solutions")
- False humility ("humble," "blessed," "honored to serve")
- Hype without proof (don't say you're the "best"—show decades of work)
- Trendy language (unless it serves a specific, clear purpose)
- Empty inspiration (no "we believe in dreaming big" without context)

### **Embrace These**
- Specifics: client names, project results, real numbers
- Honest acknowledgment of constraints or challenges
- Personality: your voice should feel like a real person, not a brand
- Parallel structure: creates rhythm and memorability
- Punctuation as emphasis: fragments. Short. Powerful.

## Voice by Channel

### **Website Copy**
Confident, direct, partnership-focused. Think: "What would we say to a potential client in a candid first conversation?"

### **About/Mission Statements**
Poetic with purpose. You can breathe a bit here. Balance the practical (what you do) with the meaningful (why it matters).

### **People/Culture Content**
Warm, inclusive, genuine. Show real lives and real passion—not just "team photos." Acknowledge diversity of backgrounds and pursuits beyond work.

### **Case Studies & Work**
Let the work speak, but frame it with strategy and insight. Start with the challenge, show your thinking, land on results. Your voice should support, not overshadow, the work.

### **News & Announcements**
Straightforward and energetic. No false drama. Lead with the why, not the announcement itself.

## Red Flags (Voice Breakers)

🚩 Overuse of adjectives ("innovative," "creative," "transformative")  
🚩 Passive voice ("Awards were won by our team...")  
🚩 Clichés disguised as insights  
🚩 Self-congratulation without context  
🚩 Talking *about* clients instead of *with* them  
🚩 Industry jargon without explanation  
🚩 Tone inconsistency (can't be "thought leaders" one sentence and warm partners the next)

## Voice Checklist Before Publishing

- [ ] Does this sound like something a real person at Davis Elen would say?
- [ ] Is it specific? (Names, numbers, real examples)
- [ ] Does it lead with what matters to the reader?
- [ ] Have I avoided corporate jargon?
- [ ] Is the tone consistent with our brand personality?
- [ ] Would this make sense to someone outside advertising?
- [ ] Does it feel honest?
- [ ] Have I shown, not just told?

## Historical Voice Evolution

**2021 Version:** "Thinking That Matters"  
Focus on deep partnership, understanding client uniqueness, passion beyond work, decades of expertise.

**2023 Version:** "Think Inside the Box"  
Evolved to a punchier, more paradoxical approach while maintaining the core: results-driven work within client boundaries.

**2026 Version:** "Artificial intelligence. Human imagination."
Pivoted to embracing AI but not letting it take over because there's still a human in the driver's seat.

**Through Both:** Consistency in independence, entrepreneurial spirit, long-term client relationships, and authentic people-focus.

## Final Thought

Davis Elen's voice is the sound of a business that's been around long enough to know what matters, independent enough to say it, and confident enough to do it without explaining itself to death.
